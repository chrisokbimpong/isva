Navigation

Appropriate security controls to implement for a medical system to prevent exposure of personal information, modification of data or the accessibility of data based on various security controls.

Integrity controls

These controls help mitigate the risk of modification of data by implementing policies or procedures to protect health information from improper or unauthorized alteration or destruction. E.g. identifying all users who have been authorized to access the system, identifying any possible unauthorized sources that may be able to intercept the information and modify it.

Access controls

These controls mitigate the risk of disclosure of personal information and availability of data by, implementing controls or policies for electronic information systems that maintain health information to allow access to persons or programs that have been granted access right and deny access to those that do not.

Person or entity authentication controls

Mitigates the risk of disclosure of personal information by, implementing controls or procedures to verify the identity of the persons or entity seeking access to the health information system.

Facility Access controls

Mitigates the risk of disclosure of personal information and availability of data by, implementing controls or procedures that limit the physical access to health information systems and the facilities they are housed, and ensure that properly authorized personals are allowed access.

Audit controls

Mitigate the risk of modification of data, by implementing hardware, software, procedure, or protocols that record and examine the activities occurring on the health information systems in real-time.
